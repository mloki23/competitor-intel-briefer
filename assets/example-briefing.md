# Competitive Intelligence Briefing
## Market: Online Project Management Tools for Small Teams
### Generated: April 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The small-team project management space is mature but fragmented. Notion dominates mindshare among tech-savvy users, while Monday.com captures mid-market with aggressive advertising. Basecamp maintains a loyal niche with flat pricing. There's a clear gap in the market for teams that want simplicity without sacrificing collaboration — most tools have swung toward complexity to justify enterprise pricing, leaving small teams (2-10 people) underserved by bloated interfaces they'll never fully use.

---

## Competitor Profiles

### Notion
- **What they do:** All-in-one workspace combining docs, databases, wikis, and project management
- **Size:** ~3,000 employees, valued at $10B+ (Series C)
- **Target customer:** Tech-savvy teams, startups, individual knowledge workers
- **Pricing:** Free (limited), Plus $10/user/mo, Business $18/user/mo, Enterprise custom
- **Key strengths:**
  - Extreme flexibility — can build almost anything
  - Strong template ecosystem
  - AI features well-integrated
- **Key weaknesses:**
  - Steep learning curve for non-technical users
  - Performance issues with large databases
  - Can become chaotic without discipline
- **Recent moves:** Launched Notion Mail (integrated email client), expanded AI capabilities, acquired Skiff for encrypted workspace features
- **Hiring signals:** Heavy hiring in AI/ML engineering and enterprise sales — signals push upmarket

### Monday.com
- **What they do:** Work operating system with project management, CRM, and dev tools
- **Size:** ~2,000 employees, publicly traded (MNDY), ~$700M ARR
- **Target customer:** Mid-market teams (10-500 people), non-technical managers
- **Pricing:** Free (2 seats), Basic $12/seat/mo, Standard $14/seat/mo, Pro $27/seat/mo
- **Key strengths:**
  - Extremely visual and intuitive interface
  - Massive advertising budget drives brand awareness
  - Strong integrations ecosystem
- **Key weaknesses:**
  - Gets expensive quickly as team grows
  - Per-seat pricing penalises larger teams
  - Feature bloat — many features most teams never touch
- **Recent moves:** Launched monday AI assistant, expanded CRM product, new developer API platform
- **Hiring signals:** Customer success and enterprise roles dominate — retention focus

### Basecamp
- **What they do:** Simple project management and team communication
- **Size:** ~80 employees, bootstrapped, profitable
- **Target customer:** Small teams, agencies, consultancies
- **Pricing:** $349/month flat (unlimited users)
- **Key strengths:**
  - Dead simple — very low learning curve
  - Flat pricing is transparent and predictable
  - Opinionated design reduces decision fatigue
- **Key weaknesses:**
  - Limited customisation
  - No Gantt charts, time tracking, or advanced reporting
  - Smaller ecosystem — fewer integrations
- **Recent moves:** Continued "Once" product line (one-time purchase software), minimal feature additions to Basecamp itself
- **Hiring signals:** Not actively hiring — stable team, no growth signals

## Comparative Analysis

### Positioning Map

Notion positions as the "build anything" flexible workspace — powerful but complex. Monday.com targets managers who want visual dashboards and workflows without coding. Basecamp occupies the simplicity end — deliberately limited to keep things calm and focused.

### Feature Comparison

| Feature | Notion | Monday.com | Basecamp |
|---------|--------|-----------|----------|
| Task management | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Docs/Wiki | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ (limited) |
| Time tracking | ❌ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Gantt charts | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Built-in chat | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| AI features | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ |
| Mobile app | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| API | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Flat pricing | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

### Pricing Comparison (10-person team, monthly)

| | Notion | Monday.com | Basecamp |
|---|--------|-----------|----------|
| Entry tier | $100/mo | $120/mo | $349/mo |
| Mid tier | $180/mo | $140/mo | $349/mo |
| Full features | $180/mo | $270/mo | $349/mo |

Basecamp is cheapest for teams above ~25 users. Below that, Notion and Monday.com have lower entry points.

## Strategic Recommendations

### Market Gaps
- **Simple + affordable for tiny teams (2-5 people):** Basecamp is simple but $349/mo is steep for a 3-person team. Notion is cheap but complex. Nothing nails both.
- **Industry-specific project management:** All three are horizontal. Vertical solutions for specific industries (agencies, trades, consultancies) could win on relevance.
- **Async-first collaboration:** Basecamp understands this philosophically but their tooling hasn't evolved. There's room for a modern async-first tool.

### Threats to Watch
- Notion's push into enterprise could fragment their product, creating an opening at the SMB end
- Monday.com's AI assistant could make their interface simpler, neutralising the complexity complaint

### Quick Wins (This Week)
1. Sign up for free tiers of all three and document the onboarding experience — friction points are product opportunities
2. Search "[competitor] complaints reddit" for each and compile a list of unmet needs
3. Check their job boards — what they're hiring for tells you where they're investing

### Long-term Plays (3-6 Months)
1. Build a focused tool that does 20% of what Notion does but for a specific audience — and make the onboarding take 5 minutes, not 5 hours
2. Adopt flat pricing from day one — per-seat pricing is the #1 complaint across all three competitors
